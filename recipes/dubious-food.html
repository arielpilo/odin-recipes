Dubious Food

The ugly brother of rock-hard food. Although hard to look or even smell at, this is a great source of energy.

Made from high quality ingredients found in the wild, and of course, love. It might not look edible, but eating it won't hurt...probbably.

Ingredients

  • Fish of your preference. I went with an elegant mighty carp.
  • One bokoblin horn. Cruelty free, please.
  • Two crikets. These two are optional, but add a nice crunch to the dish. You can fin these under rocks or hoppin around Hyrule.

These ingredients are very easy to get. You can go to your nearest pond a find some fish. Fight any bokoblin to the death and get its horn.

And if you want to add some extra crunchiness to your dish, you can cut some grass or lift some rocks and you'll sure find some crickets.

Instructions

  1. Get the biggest a nicest fish from any pond and throw that bad boy into that pot.
  2. After the fish stops moving, inside the pot add a bokoblin horn to add some crispiness to the dish.
  3. Sprinkle two dry crickets on top to add a nice fragance and seasoning. This step is optional, but will add a lot of flavor to our dish.
  4. Wait until your dish gets a censored look and texture and serve!
